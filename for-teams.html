The Seven Pillars of Our Training

A methodology born on film sets. Brought to the boardroom.

Somatic Grounding

Calm Under Pressure

The nervous system doesn't distinguish between a difficult conversation and a physical threat. This pillar trains the body's response to pressure — so when the moment arrives, calm is a practised state, not a lucky accident.

Inner Authority

Confidence Under Scrutiny

The difference between confidence that holds and confidence that collapses under pressure. Leaders learn to access a settled, grounded state under scrutiny — so what the room sees isn't a performance of confidence but the real thing.

Vocal Presence

Find Your Voice

Most people don't realise how much their voice gives away before they've said a word. This work trains a fuller, more grounded vocal quality — one that carries weight and conviction without strain or effort.

Applied Agility

Adapt Fluidly

Responding to what's actually in the room, not what was planned for. Using structured improvisation, leaders practise dropping rigid agendas and reading what's actually happening — rather than defending a position that no longer serves the moment.

Decoding the Subtext

Reading the Room

Most of what matters in a negotiation, a difficult conversation or a brief goes unsaid. This pillar trains the ability to read what's beneath the surface — the want behind the stated position, the dynamic shaping the exchange.

Command and Dynamic Presence

Own the Room

How you physically occupy space, hold attention and shape the energy of a room — before you've said a word. Not static authority — active, alive, impossible to ignore.

Physical Intelligence

Navigate the Space

Where you stand, how you move, where your attention lands — these are choices, not accidents. This pillar trains physical awareness so that every moment in a room is considered rather than unconscious.
